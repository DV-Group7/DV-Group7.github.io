INTRODUCTION

Since 1996, the municipality of Trento has been carrying out surveys of green areas and street trees in the city. It is important to specify that the data reported dates back to censuses and surveys carried out at different times. The surveys and updates of green areas are being completed and therefore the data and gardens reported are still partial or do not entirely correspond to reality.

The dataset contains a variety of information, which can be divided into two macro-categories: the first category refers to the physical characteristics of individual trees, such as species, height, crown size and position. The second, on the other hand, exploits precise methodologies that use the physical characteristics of the tree to estimate secondary information such as carbon dioxide captured, pollution removed, energy saved, and money saved.

Our goal is to rework the dataset and develop a series of graphical representations of the data that will allow for easier and faster reading of the findings.

FIRST ASSIGNMENT

HORIZONTAL BARCHART

The following “Bar Chart” shows a ranking of the top 20 most common tree species in the territory and their corresponding quantity. We find on the y-axis the names of the trees sorted in decreasing order by quantity and on the x-axis their number. It is possible to get additional information about a tree specie by moving the mouse over the corresponding bar: a text area is then shown with the numerical value of the selected tree's abundance and the average canopy size.

Graph I

HORIZONTAL STACKED BARCHART

The "Stacked Bar Chart" highlights the total number of trees in each neighborhood. In particular, it differentiates for each neighborhood the amounts of trees that belong to the top 5 most common species in the Trento area from the remaining species that are grouped together in a single category. We find on the y-axis the names of the neighborhoods and on the x-axis the number of trees.

Graph II

HORIZONTAL STACKED BARCHART WITH SMALL MULTIPLE

The "Stacked Bar Chart With Small Multiple" graph allows, for each neighborhood, to compare the top 5 most common tree species in the area with all the remaining species (grouped all together in "Others"). On the y-axis we find the names of the neighborhoods.

Graph III

HORIZONTAL STACKED BAR CHART WITH PERCENTAGE

The "Stacked Bar Chart 100%" shows the percentage present in each neighborhood of both the trees that belong to the top 5 most common species in the Trento area and the remaining species that are grouped together in a single category. We find on the y-axis the names of the neighborhoods while the x-axis shows the percentages.

Graph IV

WAFFLE CHARTS

Multiple "Waffle Chart" type graphs are shown, each representing a neighborhood, where only the top 5 most prevalent tree species in the Trento area are considered. For each chart, the various species are differentiated according to their percentage, allowing comparison of the spread of each species in each neighborhood.

SECOND ASSIGNMENT

HISTOGRAM

The “Histogram” is a graph that is used to represent the distribution of a dataset, that is the frequency with which values fall in intervals. In this case trees' height are related to the number of trees that possess that height. On the x-axis we find the height of the various trees and on the y-axis we find an integer value representing the number of trees.

Graph I

BOXPLOT

A BoxPlot is a method for graphically demonstrating the locality, spread and skewness groups of numerical data through their quartiles. In this case, the BoxPlot shows data of the trees' height.

Graph II

SCATTER PLOT

A “Scatter Plot” is a type of plot or mathematical diagram using Cartesian coordinates to display values for typically two variables for a set of data. In this specific case, on the x-axis we have the sizes of all the different trees in Trento, whereas on the y-axis we have the amount of C02 absorbed by each tree.

Graph III

SCATTER PLOT SMALL MULTIPLE

Considering only the top 6 most populated tree types in Trento, 6 different scatter plots are displayed, each one representing a different tree. Each plot is constructed in the following way: On the x-axis we have all the sizes of the considered tree type, whereas on the y-axis we have the amount of C02 absorbed by each tree of the specific tree type.

Graph IV

BUBBLE CHART

The "Bubble Chart" is a type of graph in which each entity represented is defined in terms of three distinct numerical parameters so that they can be compared according to their size and location. We find on the x-axis the tree size, on the y-axis the CO2 and at the end the bubble size represents the canopy size.
