INTRODUCTION

Since 1996, the municipality of Trento has been carrying out surveys of green areas and street trees in the city. It is important to specify that the data reported dates back to censuses and surveys carried out at different times. The surveys and updates of green areas are being completed and therefore the data and gardens reported are still partial or do not entirely correspond to reality.

The dataset contains a variety of information, which can be divided into two macro-categories: the first category refers to the physical characteristics of individual trees, such as species, height, crown size and position. The second, on the other hand, exploits precise methodologies that use the physical characteristics of the tree to estimate secondary information such as carbon dioxide captured, pollution removed, energy saved, and money saved.

Our goal is to rework the dataset and develop a series of graphical representations of the data that will allow for easier and faster reading of the findings.

FIRST ASSIGNMENT

HORIZONTAL BARCHART

First graph. The following “BarChart” shows a ranking of the top 20 most common tree species in the territory and their corresponding quantity. We find on the y-axis the names of the trees sorted in decreasing order by quantity and on the x-axis their number. It is possible to get additional information about a tree specie by moving the mouse over the corresponding bar: a text area is then shown with the numerical value of the selected tree's abundance and the average canopy size.

Graph I

HORIZONTAL STACKED BARCHART

Second graph. The Stacked Bar Chart highlights the total number of trees in each neighborhood. In particular, it differentiates for each neighborhood the amounts of trees that belong to the top 5 most common species in the Trento area from the remaining species that are grouped together in a single category. We find on the y-axis the names of the neighborhoods and on the x-axis the number of trees.

Graph II

HORIZONTAL STACKED BARCHART WITH SMALL MULTIPLE

Third graph. The "Stacked Bar Chart With Small Multiple" graph allows, for each neighborhood, to compare the top 5 most common tree species in the area with all the remaining species (grouped all together in "Others"). On the y-axis we find the names of the neighborhoods.

Graph III

HORIZONTAL STACKED BAR CHART WITH PERCENTAGE

Fourth graph. The "Stacked bar chart 100%" shows the percentage present in each neighborhood of both the trees that belong to the top 5 most common species in the Trento area and the remaining species that are grouped together in a single category. We find on the y-axis the names of the neighborhoods while the x-axis shows the percentages.

Graph IV

WAFFLE CHARTS

Fifth graph. Multiple "Waffle chart" type graphs are shown, each representing a neighborhood, where only the top 5 most prevalent tree species in the Trento area are considered. For each chart, the various species are differentiated according to their percentage, allowing comparison of the spread of each species in each neighborhood.
